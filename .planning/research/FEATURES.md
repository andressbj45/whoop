# Feature Landscape

**Domain:** Medical clinic website (women's health / OBGYN / primary care)
**Project:** Stella Mattina Website Rebuild
**Researched:** 2026-02-26
**Confidence:** HIGH — derived from direct analysis of existing SM site, Ask Tia design reference, and domain knowledge of medical clinic web patterns

## Differentiators

Features that set this rebuild apart from the average medical clinic WordPress site. Not universally expected, but high-value for patient conversion and search visibility.

| Feature | Value Proposition | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|------------|-------|
| AIO-structured content (FAQ sections per service) | AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) pull from FAQ-style content; clinics with structured answers surface in AI results | Medium | Add "Common Questions" or "FAQ" block to every service page with clear Q&A format in semantic HTML; do NOT use a JS accordion that hides content from crawlers |
| schema.org structured data — MedicalClinic | Google uses MedicalClinic, MedicalSpecialty, and Physician schema to power Knowledge Panels and rich results; most clinic sites skip this | Medium | JSON-LD blocks on: homepage (MedicalClinic), location pages (LocalBusiness/MedicalClinic), doctor pages (Physician), service pages (MedicalProcedure) |
| schema.org structured data — Physician | Individual doctor pages with Person/Physician schema make providers appear in "Find a Doctor" AI results | Medium | Include: name, specialty, affiliation, location, image, and board certifications |
| schema.org structured data — FAQPage | Makes FAQ blocks eligible for Google rich results and AI Overviews; must use structured data not just visible content | Low | Wrap FAQ sections with FAQPage + Question/acceptedAnswer JSON-LD |
| Open Graph + Twitter card meta | When patients share links in Facebook groups or text them, the preview card signals professionalism | Low | og:title, og:description, og:image per page |
| Warm minimal design (Ask Tia aesthetic) | Most medical clinic sites look clinical and cold; a warm, clean visual identity makes patients feel seen and safe before they even book | High | This is the design effort — Apply SM brand colors/logo with Ask Tia's minimal layout, soft typography, generous whitespace |
| Doctor profile linked to booking | Direct path from "Meet Dr. X" to booking with Dr. X specifically reduces friction | Low | Booking URL already exists per doctor in practitioners.json |
| Location-specific service pages | "Gynecologist in Arlington TX" is a distinct search from "gynecologist Dallas"; location-scoped service pages capture geo-targeted intent | High | e.g., /gynecology/arlington — requires matrix of services x locations; prioritize high-traffic combos |
| Condition/symptom landing pages | Patients search "irregular periods dallas" not "gynecology dallas"; condition-focused pages intercept patients earlier in their search journey | High | SM blog already contains condition content; some can be repurposed as pillar pages; requires editorial strategy |
| Image alt text + medical photography with context | Patients who see real clinic photos (doctors, staff, warm office spaces) convert better; alt text also surfaces images in Google/AI search | Low | Use existing SM photography; write descriptive alt text for every image |
| Core Web Vitals optimization | Google uses CWV as a ranking factor; Next.js is well-positioned but requires intentional image + font handling | Medium | next/image for all photos, font subsetting, no large layout shifts |
| Breadcrumb navigation + BreadcrumbList schema | Helps patients understand where they are on multi-level sites (location > service); also eligible for Google breadcrumb rich results | Low | Implement on location subpages and service pages |
| Article schema on blog posts | Blog posts with Article/MedicalWebPage schema are more likely to surface in AI Overviews and Google News | Low | JSON-LD Article block per post with author (Physician schema), datePublished, dateModified |
| Canonical URLs | Prevents duplicate content penalties from near-duplicate location pages | Low | Set per-page canonical via Next.js metadata API |
| Author bio on blog posts (with Physician schema) | Google E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust) is critical for medical content; authored posts from real MDs outrank anonymous content | Medium | Display doctor name, credentials, photo on each post; link to their provider profile page |
| Patient testimonials / trust signals section | Social proof is especially important in healthcare; patients read reviews before booking | Low | Pull existing reviews (if available); or create a static testimonial section with curated quotes |

## Anti-Features

Features to deliberately NOT build for this project — either because they're out of scope, introduce unnecessary complexity, or carry disproportionate risk.

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Custom appointment booking system | Requires HIPAA-compliant infrastructure, server-side processing, patient data storage, ongoing security maintenance — massive scope creep | Link to existing ecwcloud patient portal for all booking |
| Patient portal / login | Same HIPAA compliance burden as booking; requires auth, session management, data handling | External portal handles this entirely |
| Live chat widget | Adds third-party JS weight (CWV impact), requires real human staffing or chatbot maintenance, and creates HIPAA liability if patients share PHI | Prominent phone number + contact form is sufficient |
| In-house CMS / admin panel | First-version site is static/file-based; a CMS adds auth, DB, deployment complexity, and cost | Use file-based content (MDX or JSON); revisit CMS after site is validated |
| Insurance verification tool | Requires real-time payer API integrations; complex, expensive, high-maintenance | List accepted insurances on a static page; direct patients to call for verification |
| Symptom checker / triage tool | Clinical liability, requires medical review, extremely complex to do safely | Educational blog content addresses patient questions without clinical liability |
| Prescription refill request form | PHI collection requires HIPAA-compliant form handling and secure storage | Direct to existing patient portal |
| Patient reviews aggregation widget | Third-party JS, data freshness concerns, potential for negative review surfacing | Link to Google/Healthgrades profiles from footer; or static curated testimonials |
| Location-based auto-detection | Geolocation API adds complexity + permission friction; minimal conversion uplift vs. simple location selector | Simple locations page with all locations listed |
| Multilingual site (Spanish) | SM serves Dallas area with Spanish-speaking patients; a machine-translated site is worse than no translation and creates medical misinformation risk | If needed later, hire professional medical translator and build proper i18n — not now |
| Video consultation / telemedicine | Requires HIPAA-compliant video infrastructure, scheduling, and payment processing | Out of scope; existing portal may handle telehealth |
| AMP pages | AMP is effectively deprecated by Google in 2024+; Next.js with CWV optimization is the correct approach | Optimize Core Web Vitals instead |
| Cookie consent banner (for EU compliance) | SM targets Dallas-area patients; GDPR compliance is not required for a US-only audience | Standard US privacy policy is sufficient; if analytics require consent, use a lightweight, privacy-respecting analytics tool |

```

  → Layout components (header, footer, nav)

  → Blog listing/index page
```

## MVP Recommendation

The rebuild scope is well-defined (migrate existing site to Next.js with new design). This is not a feature-gated MVP — it's a parity rebuild + upgrade. The priority order is:

**Phase 1 — Foundation (must ship first)**
1. Global layout: header (logo, nav, phone CTA), footer (links, contact, social)
2. Homepage with hero, services overview, doctor trust signal, booking CTA
3. Contact page (phone, email, contact form if needed)
4. Privacy policy (existing content migration)

**Phase 2 — Core content pages**
5. About Us page
6. Services overview page (linking to individual service pages)
7. Individual service pages: Gynecology, Obstetrics, Hormone Therapy (Biote/Pellet), Primary Care, Women's Health, Maternal-Fetal Medicine, Immigration Physical
8. Doctor Directory listing
9. Individual doctor bio pages (all 15+)
10. Locations listing page
11. Individual location pages (all 7)

**Phase 3 — SEO + AIO layer**
12. meta tags + Open Graph on all pages
13. schema.org: MedicalClinic (homepage), LocalBusiness (locations), Physician (doctors), MedicalProcedure (services), FAQPage (service FAQs)
14. FAQ blocks on all service pages (plain semantic HTML + JSON-LD)
15. sitemap.xml + robots.txt
16. E-E-A-T: author attribution on all blog posts

**Phase 4 — Blog migration**
17. Blog listing page
18. Individual blog post pages (50+ existing posts)
19. Article + author schema per post
20. Category/tag navigation

**Defer:**
- Careers page (low patient-impact; can be a simple static page added last)
- Location-specific service pages (e.g., /gynecology/arlington) — high SEO value but high page count; prioritize after core pages are live
- Condition/symptom landing pages — editorial strategy needed; defer to post-launch

## AIO Optimization Features (Special Focus)

These features specifically target AI-powered search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews):

| Feature | How It Helps AI Search | Where to Apply |
|---------|----------------------|----------------|
| FAQ sections with clear Q&A | AI tools extract structured Q&A to answer user queries; clinics with FAQs surface as cited sources | Every service page; homepage; about page |
| FAQPage JSON-LD schema | Machine-readable signal that content is structured Q&A; eligible for Google AI Overviews and rich results | Every page with FAQ blocks |
| Plain-language service descriptions | AI summarizes clinic offerings; descriptions with clear "We offer X for Y patients" patterns are extractable | Service pages, homepage hero |
| Condition + treatment language | Patients ask AI "what is hormone pellet therapy"; pages that explain conditions + SM's approach appear as cited sources | Service pages, blog posts |
| Author + credential signals (E-E-A-T) | AI tools prefer citing authoritative, expert-authored medical content; MD-authored posts outrank anonymous content | Blog posts, service pages |
| Physician schema | AI systems use structured data to identify and cite medical professionals | Doctor bio pages |
| MedicalClinic schema | Tells AI tools what type of institution SM is, what specialties it covers, and where it's located | Homepage, location pages |
| Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) | Local AI search and map-based AI tools verify clinic identity through consistent NAP across the site | Footer, contact page, location pages |
| Internal linking between conditions and services | AI crawlers follow link topology; connecting blog post about "PCOS" to the Gynecology service page builds topical authority | Blog posts → service pages; service pages → related blog content |
