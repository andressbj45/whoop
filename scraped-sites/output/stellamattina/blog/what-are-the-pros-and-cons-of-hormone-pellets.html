What Are the Pros and Cons of Hormone Pellets?

June 06, 2024 ·By Dr. Theodore J. T. Krum

In this post, we’re looking into the pros and cons of hormone pellets, especially focusing on bioidentical pellets.

Among available hormone replacement methods, hormone pellets have a unique delivery system. And the use of bioidentical hormones means they’re even more special.

So what are bioidentical hormones?

Bioidentical hormones derive from plant sources, like soy and yams, and match the molecular structure of human hormones exactly. This close match means they’re likely to agree with your body.

And because they mimic natural hormonal activity more closely, bioidentical hormones also ensure efficient utilization and more stable hormone levels. You absorb what you need when you need it. This reduces certain unwanted side effects like fluid retention, fatigue, and mood swings.

Bioidentical hormones are therefore one of the best natural estrogen replacement options available.

Hormone pellets are tiny, solid cylinders the size of a grain of rice. Your healthcare provider implants them under your skin, typically in the hip area. The pellets contain hormones such as testosterone and estrogen, which are consistently released into the bloodstream.

This delivery method offers a convenient “set it and forget it” approach to hormone replacement. Understanding the pros and cons of hormone pellets will help you decide if this method is right for you. But first, let’s look at BioTe, the hormone pellets we use at Stella Mattina.

Biote is a specific method of hormone replacement therapy that utilizes bioidentical hormones to restore hormonal balance. The Biote method involves the insertion of customized hormone pellets tailored to your individual needs. That means we assess the current state of your hormones before deciding the exact amounts of which hormone will rebalance your system.

This approach provides a natural and consistent hormone release, minimizing fluctuations often associated with other methods of delivery.

Hormones Delivered When You Need Them

Unlike creams or patches that cause your hormone levels to fluctuate, as mentioned, hormone pellets provide your body with hormones delivered directly to your bloodstream at the time you need them biologically. This consistency helps to avoid a rollercoaster of hormonal swings.

Convenience

Once inserted, and after about four to six weeks, hormone pellets work continuously for 3 to 6 months, depending on your metabolism and the hormone used. This makes them a convenient option if you’re looking for a low-maintenance solution to hormone replacement. You won’t have to remember to replace patches or take pills.

Natural and Efficient Hormone Replacement

Bioidentical hormones in pellets present a natural alternative to synthetic hormones. Biote hormone replacement therapy is an example of this approach, providing a balanced and effective treatment.

Testosterone Pellets for Females

The benefits of testosterone pellets for females include improved libido, increased muscle mass, strength, and a general sense of well-being. It’s important to tailor the dosage to avoid side effects such as excessive hair growth or deepening of the voice. But pellets are compounded to provide exactly the right amount of testosterone for you.

Surgical Risks

The insertion of hormone pellets involves a minor surgical procedure that must be performed by a healthcare professional. There’s a small risk of infection, bleeding, or bruising at the insertion site.

Difficult to Adjust Dosage

Once hormone pellets are inserted, adjusting the dosage is not as straightforward as other forms of hormone therapy like pills or creams. If the dose is too high or too low, you’d have to wait for the effects of the pellets to diminish or have them surgically removed.

This is why your healthcare provider always assesses your hormone levels first.

Not Suitable for Everyone

Who is not a candidate for hormone pellets?

If you suffer from certain health conditions, such as active cancer or liver disease, or you’re pregnant, you may not be a suitable candidate.

Common and Rare Side Effects

While the steady release of hormones via pellets reduces fluctuations that can lead to mood swings or inconsistent symptom relief, side effects can still occur. Weight gain, changes in hair growth patterns, or more significant risks like increased blood clotting can be of concern.

Always talk to your doctor before undergoing hormone therapy. They know your medical history and can advise.

Again, discuss with your healthcare provider the pros and cons of hormone pellets for long-term use.

They’ll help you balance the benefits of sustained symptom relief against potential risks such as diminished natural hormone production. Regular monitoring and timely adjustments are crucial to maintaining the safety and effectiveness of the therapy.

While hormone pellets are not directly FDA-approved, the hormones used within them, such as estradiol and testosterone, are FDA-regulated.

Providers such as BioTe must comply with stringent manufacturing practices to ensure each batch of pellets is of high quality and safety.

Above all, as you consider the pros and cons of hormone pellets, never be tempted by online offers of kits or pellets.

Typically, the initial setup might include a comprehensive hormonal panel, consultation fees, and the cost of the pellets themselves. Regular follow-ups and subsequent pellet insertions also add to the overall expense.

Hormone pellet therapy may not be covered by all insurance plans, particularly if it’s considered elective or not the standard treatment approach. Make sure to check with your insurance provider to understand what aspects of your treatment are covered, if any.

Now you understand the pros and cons of hormone pellets you can start making an informed decision about whether hormone pellet therapy might be right for your health needs.

Moreover, consulting with doctors who specialize in women’s health makes it easier to feel understood and have trust that you’ll receive the most appropriate treatment.
