Immigration Physical

·By Dr. Theodore J. T. Krum

If you’re applying for permanent residency in the US, you’ll need a medical exam completed by a USCIS-designated Civil Surgeon as part of the green card application process.

If you’re unsure whether you need an immigration physical, our team can help you confirm before your appointment.

Our Civil Surgeon provides your immigration physical exams in a calm, supportive clinical setting.

We then document the exam on Form I-693 to help confirm you meet US health requirements for lawful permanent residency.

Once your immigration physical is complete, we prepare your Form I-693 according to USCIS instructions and give you the form in a sealed envelope for submission.

This form has to remain sealed – so please don’t open it or alter the envelope.

In some cases, results may take a short time to finalize before the form is issued.
