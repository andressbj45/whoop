How We Support Expecting Mothers from Day One

May 29, 2025 ·By Dr. Theodore J. T. Krum

As an expecting mother you want and deserve comprehensive, compassionate support – from pre-pregnancy planning to postpartum recovery.

In this article, we’ll cover all the most important questions, starting with how we can help you to get that positive test – and including:

Let’s jump straight in!

Before that “life-changing” pregnancy test turns positive, there are critical steps that can make a significant difference in your journey to becoming a mother.

If you’re trying to conceive, and success seems elusive, we provide resources and support for you. We know it can be frustrating! So, please come in for lifestyle guidance, prenatal supplements, and fertility consultations.

Why have a fertility consultation? Because fertility tests can offer valuable insight into your ovulation patterns, egg quality, and overall fertility. This helps you understand your body and optimize your chances of conception.

Once you see those two lines or a glowing “pregnant” on the test, it’s natural to feel a rush of emotions. The first step for all expectant mothers is to schedule an initial prenatal appointment with your healthcare provider.

At your first prenatal appointment, your healthcare provider will confirm the pregnancy, calculate your due date, and begin building a comprehensive care plan tailored to your unique needs.

This initial visit involves your first lab test for pregnancy – to confirm the pregnancy and check your hormone levels, including hCG, to ensure everything is progressing normally.

It’s a reasonable question to want to know what it’s “for” – especially if you’re not keen on blood draws. But blood work is a vital part of prenatal care. It provides essential information about your health and the health of your baby.

For expecting mothers, these tests can detect potential issues early, guide your personalized care plan, and offer you peace of mind at an emotional time.

They’re used for:

If you’re searching for blood work for pregnancy near me, we have same-day appointments to suit your schedule.

Your first prenatal appointment is a comprehensive visit designed to establish a strong foundation for your pregnancy care. These are the things your healthcare provider will do:

Review your medical history, which includes any previous pregnancies, existing medical conditions, and your family’s health history.

Perform a physical exam, including a pelvic exam, to assess the size of your uterus and confirm the pregnancy.

Conduct ultrasounds: Depending on how far along you are, you might have your first ultrasound to check the baby’s heartbeat and confirm your due date.

Order blood work for pregnancy: As mentioned earlier, this step is critical for assessing your overall health and detecting any potential concerns early.

Discuss prenatal vitamins and nutrition: Your provider will guide you on the best nutrients to support your baby’s growth, including folic acid, iron, and calcium.

Create a personalized prenatal appointment schedule to ensure regular check-ins throughout your pregnancy. This allows for monitoring of both your health and your baby’s development.

After your initial prenatal appointment, your provider will establish a regular schedule of check-ups to monitor your baby’s growth and your overall well-being. A typical schedule might look like this:

Weeks 4-28: Monthly visits Weeks 28-36: Bi-weekly visits Weeks 36-Birth: Weekly visits

However, recent, new guidance suggests a more personalized approach where appropriate, with possibly fewer in-person visits and other care modalities.

At Stella Mattina, we go beyond just routine prenatal care services. We understand that every pregnancy is unique, so you need comprehensive, personalized care that includes a number of things:

Personalized care plans that evolve with your pregnancy, guided by specialists who take time to understand your unique needs and preferences.

Advanced monitoring, from high-resolution ultrasounds to ongoing fetal health checks, ensures early detection of concerns and continuous peace of mind.

Genetic testing and counseling to help you understand hereditary risks and make informed decisions.

High-risk pregnancy management, with maternal fetal medicine specialists trained to handle every complication like gestational diabetes or preeclampsia.

Support for good nutrition, emotional health, and fitness.

As your pregnancy progresses, your care needs will evolve.

This includes more frequent prenatal appointments, additional blood work for pregnant women, and specialized screenings, such as glucose tolerance tests, to monitor for gestational diabetes.

We ensure that no detail is overlooked, so you can have peace of mind from the first positive test through delivery and beyond.

As your due date approaches, you’ll likely have questions about birth plans, hospital tours, and what to expect in those first precious moments with your newborn.

Don’t worry about it! We’ll guide you every step of the way.

And after childbirth, we’ll continue to support you with postpartum check-ups, lactation consulting, and personalized guidance to help you navigate breastfeeding or bottle feeding, full physical and hormonal recovery, and any challenges that may arise.

Choosing the right prenatal healthcare provider is one of the most important decisions you’ll make as a parent.

At Stella Mattina, we specialize in providing expert care focused on the needs of all women. That obviously includes you when you’re pregnant.

From fertility support to postpartum care, our experienced ObGyns in Dallas are here for you – providing the expertise you need to navigate your pregnancy with enjoyment.
