Osteoporosis is a silent yet significant health concern that affects millions worldwide, particularly postmenopausal women. However, hormone therapy for osteoporosis can offer hope if you’re looking to maintain your bone health and prevent the consequences of osteoporosis.

Understanding Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis is a condition in which your bones become brittle and fragile due to decreased bone mass and density. This deterioration can lead to fractures, especially in your hip, spine, and wrist, severely impacting your mobility and independence.

Factors contributing to osteoporosis include aging, genetics, lifestyle choices, and hormonal changes.

The Role of Estrogen and Osteoporosis

Estrogen helps regulate bone remodeling – the balance between bone formation and bone resorption. During perimenopause, your estrogen levels begin to fluctuate and eventually decline. It’s this shift that can accelerate bone loss by upsetting that balance – which makes perimenopause a good time to strengthen your bones and take preventive measures against osteoporosis!

Strengthening Your Bones During Perimenopause

Preventive measures during perimenopause strengthen bones and reduce the risk of osteoporosis. These measures could include weight-bearing exercises and ensuring adequate calcium and vitamin D intake.

Additionally, hormone therapy can strengthen your bones by compensating for declining estrogen levels. This reduces your risk of developing osteoporosis later in life.

Does Hormone Replacement Therapy Prevent Osteoporosis?

Yes! Research has shown that it can significantly improve bone density and reduce fractures if taken during perimenopause or early in post-menopause. We’re emphasizing this point about starting early to prevent any bone loss. It’s very difficult to regrow bone once it’s lost.

Hormone therapy for osteoporosis involves supplementing the body with hormones, primarily estrogen, to slow bone loss and increase bone density.

You can have this therapy in various forms, including oral tablets, patches, gels, and implants. By restoring estrogen levels, hormone therapy helps you maintain the bone remodeling balance, reducing your likelihood of fractures.

There is also recent research on the effective use of testosterone pellet therapy combined with less estrogen. Please talk to your doctor about your options.

BioTe Pellets: A Hormone Therapy Option

BioTe pellets are bioidentical hormone replacement therapy implants made from plant sources that release their hormones consistently over time. These tiny pellets are placed under the skin and provide a steady dose of estrogen. This helps to maintain optimal hormone levels without the fluctuations that can occur with other delivery methods.

Consistent hormone delivery can be particularly effective in managing symptoms of menopause and protecting your bone health.

Benefits and Risks of Hormone Therapy

While hormone therapy for osteoporosis can offer significant benefits in preserving bone density and reducing fracture risk, it’s essential to consider the potential risks.

Hormone therapy may increase the risk of certain health conditions, such as blood clots, stroke, and certain types of cancer. The risks can vary depending on factors like age, health history, and the type of hormone therapy used.

Choosing whether to pursue hormone therapy is a personal decision you should make after careful consideration and discussion with your healthcare professional. Factors to consider include:

  • Your risk factors
  • Severity of your symptoms
  • Bone density test results
  • Possible alternative treatments

So what are bioidentical hormones?

Understanding Bioidentical Hormones

Bioidentical hormones derive from plant sources, like soy and yams, and match the molecular structure of human hormones exactly. This close match means they’re likely to agree with your body.

And because they mimic natural hormonal activity more closely, bioidentical hormones also ensure efficient utilization and more stable hormone levels. You absorb what you need when you need it. This reduces certain unwanted side effects like fluid retention, fatigue, and mood swings.

Bioidentical hormones are therefore one of the best natural estrogen replacement options available.

What are Hormone Pellets?

Hormone pellets are tiny, solid cylinders the size of a grain of rice. Your healthcare provider implants them under your skin, typically in the hip area. The pellets contain hormones such as testosterone and estrogen, which are consistently released into the bloodstream.

This delivery method offers a convenient “set it and forget it” approach to hormone replacement. Understanding the pros and cons of hormone pellets will help you decide if this method is right for you. But first, let’s look at BioTe, the hormone pellets we use at Stella Mattina.

The Pros of Hormone Pellets

Hormones Delivered When You Need Them

Unlike creams or patches that cause your hormone levels to fluctuate, as mentioned, hormone pellets provide your body with hormones delivered directly to your bloodstream at the time you need them biologically. This consistency helps to avoid a rollercoaster of hormonal swings.

Once inserted, and after about four to six weeks, hormone pellets work continuously for 3 to 6 months, depending on your metabolism and the hormone used. The Cons or Risks of Hormone Pellets

Surgical Risks

The insertion of hormone pellets involves a minor surgical procedure that must be performed by a healthcare professional. There’s a small risk of infection, bleeding, or bruising at the insertion site.

Difficult to Adjust Dosage

Once hormone pellets are inserted, adjusting the dosage is not as straightforward as other forms of hormone therapy like pills or creams. If the dose is too high or too low, you’d have to wait for the effects of the pellets to diminish or have them surgically removed.

This is why your healthcare provider always assesses your hormone levels first.

If you suffer from certain health conditions, such as active cancer or liver disease, or you’re pregnant, you may not be a suitable candidate.

Always talk to your doctor before undergoing hormone therapy. They know your medical history and can advise.

Pregnancy is an extraordinary journey, but as your body adjusts to the postpartum phase, you might find yourself experiencing hormonal fluctuations. In this article, we’ll look at the changes you might notice and strategies to help you balance hormones after pregnancy – embracing natural and healthy approaches.

Signs of Hormone Imbalance After Pregnancy

Your body goes through significant hormone changes after pregnancy that can impact your physical and emotional well-being. Understanding them is important for your recovery. Let’s look at them in turn and see what symptoms they might cause. Knowledge is power!

Estrogen and Progesterone

Estrogen plays a part in various bodily functions, including bone health and skin elasticity, while progesterone supports pregnancy and then decreases postpartum. Both these hormones experience a significant drop after birth. Here are the signs to look out for:

Mood swings: The decrease in estrogen and progesterone can contribute to a rollercoaster of emotions for you, making you feel ecstatic one moment and tearful the next. You’re not losing the plot! It’s natural after pregnancy.

Fatigue: Alongside the demands of caring for a newborn, the hormone drop can make you feel more tired than usual.

Hair loss: High estrogen levels during pregnancy reduce hair shedding and keep your hair lush. A postpartum drop in estrogen can cause noticeable hair loss, which can be distressing – but the good news is it’s typically temporary.

Irregular menstrual cycles: It’s normal for your cycle to be unpredictable as it finds its new rhythm, especially if you’re breastfeeding. Try not to worry about it, because stress affects your hormones too!

Thyroid Hormones

Postpartum thyroiditis may occur and lead you to have fluctuating thyroid hormone levels that contribute to tiredness and emotional changes, mirroring those symptoms of estrogen and progesterone imbalance.

Hormones and Nursing After Pregnancy

Nursing your baby is not just a beautiful way to bond but also plays a role in your hormonal balance postpartum. Breastfeeding triggers the release of several hormones, and understanding their role will point to strategies for you to help balance hormones after pregnancy.

Prolactin

Prolactin levels increase throughout pregnancy and each time you nurse, maintaining your milk supply. It also has a calming effect, enhances maternal behaviors, and strengthens the emotional bonding with your baby.

If you’re breastfeeding, prolactin remains elevated and can influence other hormones. You may have a delayed return of regular menstrual cycles, and some women even experience amenorrhea (absence of periods) while nursing. However, this effect, while serving as a natural contraception method, is not foolproof! Ovulation may resume unpredictably. You should still take precautions to avoid a further pregnancy until you’re ready.

Oxytocin

Oxytocin, known as the “love hormone,” facilitates milk flow by contracting your milk ducts. Beyond its role in lactation, oxytocin stimulates uterine contractions, helping your uterus return to its pre-pregnancy size and reducing postpartum bleeding. The surge of oxytocin also strengthens the bond between you and the baby and supports your well-being by reducing stress and fostering a sense of calm.

However, if you’re not nursing, expect your oxytocin levels to return to normal within a couple of weeks. Likewise, when you start to wean, both prolactin and oxytocin levels will revert to normal

Balancing your Hormones After Pregnancy Naturally

Diet plays a central role in supporting recovery, so we recommend a nutrient-rich diet to help you balance hormones after pregnancy. Discuss with your healthcare provider if there are foods to avoid while breastfeeding, but in general these are healthy:

  • Complex carbohydrates (whole grains, legumes) help stabilize blood sugar levels, indirectly supporting hormonal balance.
  • High-quality proteins (lean meats, beans, nuts, seeds) are essential for hormone production and repair of body tissues.
  • Healthy fats (avocados, olive oil, nuts) support cellular function and hormone production.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, mackerel) support hormonal health, reduce inflammation, enhance brain function, and promote overall well-being.
  • Vitamin D is essential for bone health, immune function, and hormonal balance. Sun exposure and dietary sources (like fortified foods) contribute to vitamin D intake.
  • Magnesium (leafy greens, nuts, seeds) is a mineral that supports hundreds of reactions in your body, including hormone regulation.
  • Probiotics (yogurt, kefir, fermented vegetables) support a healthy gut microbiome, which in turn supports hormone regulation.

Incorporating supplements to balance hormones after pregnancy, such as vitamin D, Magnesium, and Omega-3, can give you a much-needed boost if you cannot get the amounts you need through diet and lifestyle. Before taking any supplements, make sure to discuss them with your doctor, whether you are breastfeeding or not.

When Do Your Hormones Stabilize After Pregnancy?

The timeline varies for each woman, but generally, it takes a few months for hormones to stabilize. After around six months, most women experience more stable hormone levels, with your body adapting to the demands of motherhood and hormonal fluctuations becoming less pronounced.

During these months, here are some ideas to help:

Track Your Menstrual Cycle

Returning to a regular menstrual cycle is often a sign that your hormones are starting to stabilize. Keep note of the frequency, duration, and any symptoms of your periods. Irregularities can be normal in the beginning, but tracking them can help identify patterns or issues that might need attention.

Symptom Journaling

Maintain a journal of symptoms that could indicate hormonal fluctuations, such as mood changes, energy levels, sleep quality, and skin condition. This will help you recognize patterns over time and provide concrete data to discuss with your doctor.

Consultations with Healthcare Providers

Schedule regular check-ups with your healthcare provider to discuss your progress. These sessions can be invaluable for getting professional insights into your hormonal health, adjusting any necessary treatment plans, and getting reassurance or advice on the next steps.

By monitoring your progress, you gain insights into the effectiveness of your efforts to balance hormones after pregnancy. However, don’t despair if things are not improving.
